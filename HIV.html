HIV

Retrovirus

  • enveloped in postive RNA strand
  • Causes tissue tropism, host range virion morphology and genetic complexity
  • 1) Oncovirinae: Virus can immortalize or transform target cells
  • 2) Lentivirinae: Slow viruses associated with with neurologic immunosupressiv disease
  • 3)Spumavirinae: Cause distinct cytopatholigic effect

Genes

  • Gag: Capsid,matrix & nucleic acid binding protein
  • Pol: polymerase, protease, and integrase
  • Env: envelope & glycoprotein

Pathogenesis

  • HIV induces immunosupression-> reduces CD4 T-cells and activate / control innate and immune response
  • HIV infect the mucosal surface and MALT
  • Macrophage,DC, memory T cell are reservoir
  • CD8 T cell kill the virus and limits its replication

3 types of phases

  • Acute infection: Large burst of virus production
  • Asymptomatic phase: CD8 T cell kill virus, and decreases level of virus in blood
  • Chronic infection: CD4 T cell drop and cannot maintain the amount CD8 T cell activation
