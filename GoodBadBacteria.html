Types of Bacteria

Commensal Bacteria: These Bacteria live in symbiosis with the hosts. They are present on the skin, airways and GI. Their functions are creating a barrier, producing vitamins and promote growth factor, as well they support the immune system and balance it

Opportunistic Bacteria: These bacteria do not always cause a disease but can take advantage of an existing condition

Pathogenic Bacteria: These bacteria always cause a disease.


Gram-positive vs Gram-negative

Looking at the structure of the Gram-positive bacteria on the peptidoglycan layer we find Teichoic acid, Lipoteichoic acid and surface protein, on the other hand, Gram-negative bacteria have on the outer membrane lipopolysaccharide(LPS) and surface protein, under the outer membrane, there is the lipoprotein layer and than the peptidoglycan layer. In conclusion, the Gram-positive bacteria has a thicker cell wall than Gram-negative bacteria

General Structure of Bacteria

Fimbriae

  • Filamentous protein structure
  • Helps to attach/stick on surfaces
  • Type IV is involved in twitching motility and genetic transfer

Pili

  • Filamentous protein structure
  • Longer than Fimbriae
  • Attaches as well on surfaces
  • Is able to exchange genetic information through conjugation
  • Receptor for certain Viruses
