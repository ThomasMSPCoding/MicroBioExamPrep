Mycobacteria Tuberculosis

Mechanism of action

  • Enters the respiratory airway
  • Infectious particle penetrate aveoli: Macrophage phagocyte the infection
  • Tuberculosis on the other hand interfere the fusion with phagosome and lysosome
  • Therefore it interact with other intracellular vesicles
  • This blocks the killing mechanism of macrophage and can not mediate reactive nitrogen

    • Immune sytem response

  • In response of the infection: Macrophage secrete IL-12 and TNF-alpha
  • They localize the infection and recruit T-cell and Natural Killer Cell to the infected macrophage
  • T-cell differentiate to T- helper cell and secrete IFN-gamma: Infected macrophage are activated and increase the fusion and killing mechanism
  • TNF-alpha stimulate the production of Nitrogen Oxide and Reactive Nitrogen

    • Stages of Tuberculosis

    Stage 1: Initial Macrophage Response

  • Infectious Tuberculosis droplet enters the Respiratory airways through contact to contact by person
  • Stimulate the Immune response

    • Stage 2: Growth

  • The Tuberculosis bacillus inhibits the macrophage action of mechanism(phygocytosis) and reproduce exponentially

    • Stage 3: Immune control

  • Immune cells are recruited to the infection site
  • Granulomas and epitheloid cells fight Tuberculosis
  • Primary Tuberculosis:The infection occurs on uninfected host->Ghon focus
  • Latent Tuberculosis: When Ghon focus heals, the infection enters the latent state. That makes the bacteria survive but cant replicate and patient is not contagious

    • Stage 4: Lung Cavitation

  • If the Ghon Focus does not heal, it develops into an active disease
  • Secondary Tuberculosis: Reactivation of latent Tuberculosis infection

    • Diagnostic

  • Tuberculin skin Test
  • Blood test
  • Chest X-Ray test
  • Microscopy
  • Culture
  • Nucleic Acid Based Test:PCR

    • How to treat

  • Antibiotic usage: Rifampin and Isoniazid
  • Immnuoprophylaxis: vaccination with attenuated M-Bovis

    • MDR/RR - Tuberculosis

  • Cycle not correctly ended
  • Only take when necessary
  • Combination of Antibiotic can occur to MDR
