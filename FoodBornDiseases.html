HUS is associated with Stx 2 and damages glomerular endothelial cells

Mechanism of E. coli

STEC (EHEC) - Shiga Toxin produced E. coli

  • STEC ranges from mild uncomplicated diarrhea to hemarrhagic colitis, sever abdominal pain and bloody diarrhea
  • STEC has 2 sort of toxins: Stx1 and Stx2. Both contain 1A and 5B subunit, where B subunit bind to Gb3 receptor to expose endothelial cells and A subunit binds to 28S rRNA to inhibit the protein synthesis

EPEC - Enteropathogenic E. coli

  • Must contain the gene LEE and absence of Stx
  • Bacteria bind on the cell surface of the small intestine and destroy microvillus. In initiation of EPEC, formation of microcolonies occur on epithelial cell. Toxins are injected into the host cell and create a receptor Tir to use it for bacterial adhesin. This procedure creates a loss of cell surface.

ETEC - Enterotoxigenic E. coli

  • Heat stable enterotoxin creates a hypersecretion and inhibition of reabsorption.
  • Heat- labile enterotoxin creates a enhanced secretion of chloride and decreased absorption of chloride and sodium

EAEC - Enteroaggregatic E.coli

  • Creates a stacked brick on the cell surface of the small intestine. This result in inflammatory response

EIEC - Enteroinvasiv E. coli

  • Bacteria invade and destroy colonic epithelium

What causes a food outbreak?

  • Contaminated food
  • Poor hygiene
  • Temperature
  • Cross contamination
  • Pest infestation
  • Infected worker

Terms

  • Outbreak: Designated area and specific group of people are infected
  • Epidemic: Larger area (Like county or country) that has a large amount of people infected
  • Epidemic: Global infection and all type of groups are infected

How to prevent an outbreak?

  • Education
  • Regularly inspection and controls
  • Sanitize area and worker
  • Use glows and check own hygiene

HAACP - Management system / Food inspection

    It is the analysis and inspection of hazard material, process, handling, distribution and consumption

  • 1) Conduct hazard analysis
  • 2) Determine critical control point
  • 3) Establish critical limits
  • 4) Establish corrective action
  • 5) Establish verification procedure
  • 6) Establish record keeping and documentation procedure
