General characteristics of viruses

  • Contains nucleic acids(DNA &RNA) and proteins
  • Lipids and proteins(enveloppe)
  • They must replicate in living cells
  • Replication mechansims varies in different types of viruses
  • Replication effect + Immune response = Pathogensis

Influenza virus diagram

General Traits of viral replication

  • Firstly encounter susceptible host or tissue
  • Entry of the pathogen by attachement with Hemaglutinin on sialic acid that binds to the cell surface
  • Entry into the host cell by internization with endosome in the cytoplasm (Fusion of envelope with endosome)
  • Uncoating of envelope and delivering the nucleocapsid in the cytoplasm
  • Transcription and Translation process occurs in the cell
  • Genome replication
  • Assembly occurs when nucleocapsid interacts with M1 protein
  • Exit by binding on the apical surface

    • Positive RNA don't need RNA polymerase!!!! Other RNA such as negative RNA needs viral RNA polymerase to replicate

    Flu symptoms

    • Headache, malaise, fever, muscle pain
    • Influenza may cause pneumonia
    • Can also cause super bacterial Infection

    Antigenic Drift and Antigenic shift

    • Antigenic drift:Are frequent minor mutation in virus genetic material
    • Antigenic shift:Are major alteration caused by reassortment of viral genetic material through different types of viral strains, potentially creating a new virus

    Treatment and Diagnosis

    Diagnosis

    • Cell culture: can detect the virus presence
    • Hemadosorption: Detect the presence of HA
    • Hemaglutination: Detects the virus in presence of secretion
    • Hemaglutination inhibition: Detects the type or strain of Influenza
    • ELISA / Immunofluerence
    • Serology
    • Genomics

    Treatment

    • Rimantadine: Targets the M2 protein, which inhibits the uncoating process of the virus
    • Zanamivir: Inhibits HA but if there is no HA, NA binds to the sialic acids on the cell surface and prevent the exit of the virus(making it trap in the host cell)
    • Bolaxavir marboxil: Targets the activity of RNA polymerase PB2
